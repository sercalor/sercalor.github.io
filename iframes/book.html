Published in 2016, this book is a collection of all the voices behind the creation of Conversation Room as a relational device, an organic sculpture, and an oral archive of narrative and affect. 

As a verbal spark, 'Why Do We do The Things We Do?' sets the stage for what this project is or could be about in relation to its users. It aims to provoke introspection in a collaborative environment for conversation where the stories behind our choices and the feelings behind our words collide, transform, or reflect a wider social entanglement. 

In this book, you will find texts by Nora Sternfeld and Henna Harri, who discuss how the question of 'why' creates blockage in the flow of our every-day lives. Orlan Ohtonen talks about encounters of listening as bodily interpersonal experiences, and Taru Elfving writes about the dynamics of the private and public moods and how the work mediates between them.
